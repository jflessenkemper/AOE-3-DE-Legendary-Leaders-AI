Legendary Leaders AI session notes - 2026-04-20

Scope covered in this session
- Verified that the tree should mirror the mod's actual portraits and flags rather than inferred or generated assets.
- Confirmed where base-game flags and portraits live in AoE3 DE BAR archives and extracted them for comparison.
- Researched the revolution AI architecture against standard-nation leader files to plan parity improvements for all 26 revolutions.
- Built a phased improvement plan covering flags, portraits, revolution AI, tree HTML, and content completeness.

Important completed findings
- Portrait usage was confirmed to be driven by the same cpai_avatar_*.png family used by AI personality files, civ picker bindings, and matchmaking portrait bindings.
- The tree was previously corrected to mirror the mod more closely, including the Mexico portrait/name alignment and flag-casing fixes.
- Base-game flags were extracted from Game/UI/UIResources1.bar.
- Base-game singleplayer portraits were extracted from Game/UI/UIResources4.bar.
- Straight filename-to-filename BAR extraction comparison was unreliable because the large flag payloads in the archive did not line up cleanly with the parsed names.
- A hash-based binary comparison of actual file payloads produced the usable result.

Flag audit status
- 96 mod flag files were found to be byte-identical to base-game assets.
- 18 mod flag files were identified as mod-only assets.
- Several referenced revolution/postgame large flags use casing-specific or placeholder-style files and need safe reference cleanup before duplicate asset deletion.
- Small home-city flag assets matched base-game files much more cleanly than the large flag set.

Portrait audit status
- Base-game portraits were extracted for the standard civ roster.
- The standard civs were classified into two groups:
  - same-leader cases where the base-game portrait may be reusable
  - different-leader cases where custom portraits remain necessary
- All revolution portraits still need custom treatment or explicit keep/replace decisions.

Revolution AI parity findings
- Standard nations generally use dedicated leader XS files with age-based progression, uncapped pop settings, and more specific doctrine behavior.
- Most revolutions still route through shared revolution commander logic with doctrine buckets, simpler triggers, and hard army/civilian caps.
- Three revolutions already have bespoke leader files: Napoleon, Washington, and Hidalgo.
- The remaining revolution nations still need dedicated leader-file treatment if they are to reach parity with standard nations.
